# Phase 9 — Pitch Script

## 0:00-0:20

University hardship meal support is a real operational problem. Student Affairs has to issue support, merchants have to redeem it quickly, and auditors need a trustworthy history.

## 0:20-0:40

The failure is simple: eligibility, revocation, and redemption are spread across different actors, so duplicate redemption and stale status are hard to control with one editable list or one department-owned database.

## 0:40-1:00

We built a wallet-free QR voucher flow for one campus. The student only shows a QR pass, the merchant verifies and redeems it, and the auditor sees a shared event history.

## 1:00-1:20

Blockchain is used only where the trust problem actually lives: revocation, redemption state, and audit checkpoints. We do not put student identity, hardship reasons, or payment settlement on-chain.

## 1:20-1:40

In the demo, the first redemption succeeds and the second identical redemption is blocked. That is the core value: one shared state across Student Affairs, merchants, and auditors.

## 1:40-2:00

This can pilot on one campus with one Student Affairs office and one or two cafeterias. It is responsible by design, practical for non-crypto users, and scoped tightly enough to ship as a real campus workflow.
