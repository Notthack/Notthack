# Phase 7 — Win Strategy

## Strategic Positioning

Do not sell this as an aid app.  
Sell it as a `shared trust layer` for a university hardship meal support workflow.

## Winning Thesis

`Student Affairs, campus merchants, and auditors need one trustworthy redemption state.`

That is the story. Everything else is support.

## What To Emphasize

- real institution, not a toy problem
- wallet-free student flow
- duplicate redemption prevention
- auditor-visible history
- off-chain eligibility, on-chain shared trust state
- one-campus pilot path

## What To De-Emphasize

- any language that sounds like a generic voucher dashboard
- blockchain internals
- smart contract mechanics
- any implication that eligibility is decided at checkout
- anything beyond the frozen MVP

## Judgment Strategy

When speaking to judges:

1. start with the real operational failure
2. show why normal software leaves an audit gap
3. show the QR flow
4. show the duplicate block
5. show the auditor history
6. end on deployment path and responsible design

## Core Win Condition

The judges should leave with one sentence in their head:

`This is a normal-looking campus workflow where blockchain is used only where shared trust actually matters.`

## Competition Risk

The biggest risk is not technical failure.  
The biggest risk is sounding generic.

## Countermeasure

Use concrete wording:

- not `voucher platform`
- not `aid dashboard`
- not `donation transparency`
- instead `shared redemption and revocation state across Student Affairs, merchants, and auditors`
