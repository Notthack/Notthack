# Phase 0 — Sponsor Reverse Engineering

## Sponsor Summary

1. BGA is not rewarding blockchain novelty on its own; it is positioning blockchain as public-interest infrastructure for trust, accountability, and measurable impact.
2. Its public messaging and 2025 awards signal strong preference for problems in digital public goods, sustainability, financial inclusion, and digital trust, especially where multiple institutions must coordinate.
3. BGA appears to like solutions that can move into incubation, pilots, or partnerships after the hackathon, not one-off demo toys.
4. Winning examples are not wallet-first consumer apps. They are systems for verification, traceability, transparent distribution, and institutional auditability.
5. For this track, “good blockchain use” means backend trust, proof, revocation, or audit. It does not mean making normal users touch wallets.

## Judging Summary

Derived from the BGA track brief, local workflow, and public BGA messaging:

- First filter: is the problem real, specific, and tied to identifiable users or institutions?
- Second filter: does blockchain solve a trust, coordination, audit, or revocation problem better than a normal shared system?
- Third filter: is the deployment path believable within an NGO, government, school, verifier, exporter, or small business context?
- Fourth filter: is the impact easy to explain in one sentence?
- Fifth filter: can a non-crypto user complete the flow without chain knowledge?

## Anti-Patterns

- Generic donation transparency.
- Wallet-first UX for ordinary users.
- Token mechanics without a real trust reason.
- “Marketplace + NFT + sustainability” with no institution in the loop.
- Broad social-good narratives without one narrow user flow.
- Blockchain used as a badge rather than a trust mechanism.
- Demos that depend on many integrations or real liquidity.

## Likely Winning Pattern Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1

The strongest concepts will be institutional workflows where at least 3 actors need a shared, tamper-resistant record.

### Hypothesis 2

Winners will keep blockchain mostly invisible to the end user and expose it only at the proof, redemption, or audit moment.

### Hypothesis 3

Judges will reward concepts that can name a first pilot partner category immediately, such as municipality, NGO, verifier, school, or SME exporter.

### Hypothesis 4

Ideas that reduce fraud, duplicate claims, fake documents, or unverifiable sustainability claims will outperform “engagement” ideas.

### Hypothesis 5

The best stories will show a real-world workflow failure first, then explain blockchain in one tight sentence as the minimum trust layer.

## Public Winner Signals

### Strong positive signals

- `Genius Tags`, `Plastiks`, `ZenGate` were recognized in BGA's 2025 SDG accelerator context. These all point toward traceability, verification, sustainability, and multi-party trust.
- `Rahat` is an open-source cash and voucher assistance platform for vulnerable communities with end-to-end transparency for humanitarian agencies. This is highly aligned with BGA's “real users + auditability + non-crypto UX” preference.
- `Token Tails`, `Rumsan`, and `eSusFarm` suggest BGA is open to different sectors, but still expects an operational system with measurable field outcomes.

### Negative inference

There is no visible evidence that BGA's flagship examples are rewarding speculative DeFi mechanics, loyalty tokens, or consumer wallet education as the core value proposition.

## Recommendation To Use In Later Phases

Optimize for:

- real institutional pain
- one narrow user flow
- backend blockchain only
- clear audit or revocation point
- pilot partner plausibility

Avoid:

- donation platforms
- crypto onboarding
- token-first concepts
- ideas whose blockchain can be deleted without changing trust assumptions

## Assumptions

- The exact internal scoring weights are not public in machine-readable form, so weighting is inferred from BGA's published judging language and 2025 award patterns.
- Public winner names are used as directional signals, not as proof of exact judging weights.
